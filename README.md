# Alex Teplov, CPA

I build AI agent workflows for accounting practices.

My work sits across two specialized areas of Canadian practice: self-employed professionals and e-commerce sellers. The workflows I build come from real client files and real recurring work inside a firm. They focus on where AI can handle the repeatable parts reliably, and where a CPA still needs to step in to catch what the model misses.

Most AI tools in accounting are built by people who understand LLMs. These are built by someone who also does the month-end.

## Repositories

### [cpa-skills](https://github.com/alexteplovcpa/cpa-skills)
Practice-tested AI workflow skills for CPAs and bookkeepers.

This repository is practitioner-facing. It is built around how accounting work actually moves through a practice: intake, document organization, review, follow-up, issue spotting, and tax preparation support.

### [it-contractors-skills](https://github.com/alexteplovcpa/it-contractors-skills)
Client-facing tax preparation workflows for Canadian IT contractors and consultants.

This repository helps clients gather records, organize income and expenses, identify missing items, and arrive at CPA review with a cleaner file. It covers both sole proprietors and incorporated contractors.

### [ecommerce-skills](https://github.com/alexteplovcpa/ecommerce-skills)
Accounting workflows for Canadian e-commerce sellers.

This repository focuses on marketplace reconciliation, inventory and COGS treatment, GST/HST handling, multi-platform reporting, and seller-specific bookkeeping issues.

## Focus

I am interested in a simple question: where does AI actually help inside an accounting practice, and where does it still need a CPA to review, correct, or decide?

That is what these repositories are for.
